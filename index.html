Our recommendation: Chamonix. Best value accommodation (€130–250/night), only 1 hour from Geneva (shortest drive of all options), a real Alpine town with year-round restaurants and nightlife — not a purpose-built resort. The Vallée Blanche is a once-in-a-lifetime 22 km glacier descent. For the Three Valleys option, Val Thorens offers the best snow guarantee and most affordable lodging, but it's 2h15 from Geneva and purpose-built.

The Three Valleys are fully interconnected by lifts — one pass, ski between Courchevel, Méribel, and Val Thorens without removing skis. Chamonix is different: 5 ski areas connected by free bus/train, but only 1h from Geneva.
🇫🇷 Three Valleys → Geneva
~2h15 drive. All 3 valleys interconnected. One pass = 600 km.
🇫🇷 Chamonix → Geneva ★
~1h drive. Shortest transfer. Real town. 5 ski areas by bus.
🇨🇭 Verbier → Geneva
~1h45 drive. Swiss 4 Vallées. 410 km. Famous freeride.
